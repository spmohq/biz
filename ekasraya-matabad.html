Ekasraya Matabad - The Doctrine of the One Shelter - My Life Realization

I want to share my life’s realization here, simply and openly, for anyone who feels called to read.

I do not claim to have discovered a new truth.

I simply share what life has revealed to me.

Just as a tenant lives under a roof and follows certain rules, I see my life — our lives — as tenants sheltered under the vast home of this universe.

I call this realization Ek캐콑raya M캐t캐b캐d — The Doctrine of the One Shelter.

What is it? Here it is:

Just as a tenant must pay rent and follow the terms agreed upon before moving in, your life, too, is a tenant — living within the shelter of this universe.

You owe it rent, and you are bound by the terms and conditions the universe has laid down for all its creatures, including you.

So, what are these terms and conditions?

The universe asks only one thing: help maintain its balance and harmony — the living ecosystem that keeps everything alive and moving forward. This balance is what drives life, growth, and peace.

游 The first rule is simple: whatever you do, do it in a way that preserves and nurtures the harmony of the universe. Do not block its natural flow — be a force that sustains it.

游꺔 The second rule is a reminder: the work you do to earn your living directly affects this balance. You are not the ultimate doer. You are a worker under the true doer. The universe’s own Karma Yoga is the real force behind all action.

游깶 The third rule is humbling: as a worker, you are entitled to your fair wages — your share for your effort — but the true owner of the result is the universe itself. We, the workers, come and go, but the universe remains and retains the fruit of every action so its cycle continues.

The first rule is your compass — your guiding star.

The other two rules teach you how to follow that star with purpose and dignity.

So, live like a responsible tenant: honour your rent, respect the conditions, and care for the home you live in — this timeless universe — so it stays alive and abundant for all who come after you.

This is my life’s realization.

I share it only to remind myself — and anyone who reads it — that balance, harmony, peace, and honest work are our true rent for living under One Shelter.
